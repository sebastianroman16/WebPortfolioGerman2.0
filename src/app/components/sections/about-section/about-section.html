About Me

I am from Santiago, Chile. I attended a high school that specialized in electricity as a trade, so when I graduated, I also became a certified electrician. In my final year, I completed an internship, which led to my first job.

After high school, I pursued a degree in automation and industrial control, graduating in 2013 as the valedictorian of my program. I then worked in the electrical field for five years across various factories and companies.

In 2015, I decided to continue my education and began studying electrical engineering while finding a job that accommodated my new schedule. That same year, I got married and started the process of becoming a U.S. resident.

I received my residency before completing my engineering degree, so I decided to move to the U.S. in 2017. In 2018, while working, I studied to obtain my HVAC license and later secured a job as an HVAC technician.

After completing the HVAC program, I was offered a position as an instructor at the same professional technician school I attended due to my advanced knowledge of electricity. I worked there for two years, teaching the basics of electricity and HVAC.

I worked as an HVAC technician for four years and decided to return to school in 2021 to pursue an Associate of Arts degree. In 2022, I became a full-time student, and in 2023, I graduated.

In 2024, I transferred to the University of South Florida (USF), where I am currently completing my bachelor's degree in electrical engineering.
