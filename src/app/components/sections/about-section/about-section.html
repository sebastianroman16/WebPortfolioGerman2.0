About Me

I am an Electrical Engineering student with extensive hands-on experience in electrical systems, automation, and industrial control. I began my technical training in Santiago, Chile, where I attended a trade-focused high school specializing in electricity and became a certified electrician, completing an internship that led to my first professional role.

I later earned an associate's degree in Automation and Industrial Control, graduating as valedictorian, and worked for several years in industrial and factory environments performing electrical installation, troubleshooting, and control-related tasks. After relocating to the United States, I continued working in the electrical field while further expanding my technical scope by completing an HVAC program, earning both a diploma and professional license, and working as an HVAC technician. I was later offered an instructor position at a professional technical school, where I taught electrical and HVAC fundamentals.

I am currently a Student Assistant at the University of South Florida, where I support the Automation and Controls Laboratory and assist with laboratory-based courses. I was selected for this role after successfully completing a research project in which I built, commissioned, and brought into operation the first delta robot at USF (Omron Quattro 650HS).
